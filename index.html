About

Ayesha is a computer science and economics student at Melbourne University, Australia.

For her work in developing a robotics curriculum for high schools in the UAE and spreading technological education in Australia, she was the only Australian recipient of the inaugural Global Impact Scholarship 2020 from Palantir, where she was selected as 1 of 5 globally. She was also the youngest recipient of the Google WTM Scholarship 2019 in the APAC region.

Ayesha interned at Google in Sydney under Google Maps in 2019 and will be rejoining them in 2020. She has also dabbled in web development, ML engineering, research software engineering, and medical robotics through work experience during her degree.

Ayesha enjoys engaging with Australia's tech community and regularly hosts talks and events on AI, competitive programming, quantum computing, and ethics in tech. She hosted Australia's first Google Hash Code @ Melbourne Uni in 2019 and is passionate about the role technology plays in enriching society.

Ayesha looks up to the work of AI & society experts Karen Hao and Rumman Chawdhury . She is currently actively working on an iniative to promote societal awareness and ethics of technological impact in society. More details TBD early 2021.
