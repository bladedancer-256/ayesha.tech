About

"We shouldn't wait to observe the impact of technology; we should be anticipating them. And that means tech and social experts shouldn't be on opposite sides, they should be together, at the very beginning." -Karen Hao

Ayesha is a computer science and economics student at The University of Melbourne, Australia.

She was the only Australian recipient of Palantir's inaugural Global Impact Scholarship 2020, from a total group of 5. This was in recognition of her developing a robotics curriculum for high schools in the UAE and spreading technological education in Australia. She was also the youngest recipient of the Google WTM Scholarship 2019 in the APAC region.

In 2019, Ayesha interned at Google Sydney in the Maps team and will be rejoining them in 2020. She is excited about engineering, having dabbled in ML engineering, research software engineering, medical robotics, and web development through work experience during her degree.

Ayesha is highly engaged with Australia's tech community and regularly hosts talks on AI, competitive programming, quantum computing, and ethics in tech. She hosted Australia's first Google Hash Code @ Melbourne Uni in 2019.

Ayesha believes that technology is more than its technical underpinning, encompassing social and economical problems as well. She looks up to AI & society experts Karen Hao and Rumman Chowdhury , whose work follow a similar paradigm.

She is currently working on an initiative to promote societal awareness of technological impact and methods for others to enhance its development. More details to come early 2021.
